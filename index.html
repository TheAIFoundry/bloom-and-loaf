Why Choose Sourdough?

Naturally Fermented. Better for You.

Our sourdough is made the traditional way — with a natural starter, long fermentation, and only three simple ingredients: flour, water, and salt.

  • No commercial yeast
  • No preservatives
  • No additives

Just time and craft.

Easier to Digest

Through slow fermentation, wild yeast and beneficial bacteria begin breaking down gluten and complex starches before the bread ever reaches your oven.

Many people find sourdough feels lighter and easier on the stomach compared to conventional bread.

Balanced Energy

Because sourdough ferments slowly, it has a lower glycemic impact than standard yeast bread. That means steadier energy and less of the crash that can follow highly processed breads.

Better Flavor

Long fermentation develops depth and complexity you simply can't rush.

The result: a crisp crust, a tender crumb, and that subtle tang that makes real sourdough unforgettable.

Meet the Baker

The heart behind Bloom & Loaf

Alyson Truong

Founder & Baker

For Alyson, it all started in the garden. What began as a love of growing things — nurturing seeds into blooms, tending soil with patience and care — quietly found its way into the kitchen. The same hands that coax flowers from the earth now shape dough, feed starters, and score loaves by hand.

Sourdough felt like a natural extension of that love: something living, something slow, something that rewards attention and care. When she's not elbow-deep in flour, you'll likely find her in the garden, dreaming up new recipes, or being followed around by a very enthusiastic golden retriever.

Bloom & Loaf is where her two passions meet — flowers and fermentation, patience and craft, all baked into every loaf she makes.
